Book Reviews

What my students have said about the book...

A must buy for every aspiring musician

This book explores the science behind why music affects us emotionally and intellectually. Levitin, a neuroscientist and musician, breaks down concepts like rhythm, melody, and harmony in a way that’s engaging for both musicians and casual listeners. Some readers find it a bit technical, but overall, it’s a fascinating look at how our brains process music.

Review by Daniel J. Levitin
How Music Works

Written by Talking Heads frontman David Byrne, this book dives into the evolution of music, how technology and environment shape it, and the economics of the music industry. It’s part memoir, part music theory, and part social commentary. Fans love the way Byrne’s passion for music shines through, though some wish it had a bit more structure.

Review by David Byrne
Nailed it!

Neurologist Oliver Sacks tells incredible stories of people whose lives have been changed by music, from those who develop musical abilities after brain injuries to individuals who experience music hallucinations. It’s a deep and touching exploration of music’s power, though some readers find the medical aspects a bit dense.

Review by Mike
